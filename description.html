DAMMA • ደማ

Official Game Specification & Rules Guide
Origin: Ethiopia (Horn of Africa) Category: Abstract Strategy Submission Standard: A4 Format

1. Historical & Cultural Context [01]

Damma (ደማ) is a traditional mathematical abstract board game deeply rooted in the cultural heritage of the Horn of Africa, particularly Ethiopia. Played for centuries on custom-carved wooden blocks, stones, or directly in the dirt of rural village gathering sites, Damma fosters tactical computation, spatial reasoning, and communal bonding. It belongs to the broad draughts/checkers family of games but features highly dynamic, aggressive jump sequences and the prestigious "Damma" (Flying King) promotion, requiring extreme foresight and positional dominance.

2. Board Representation [02]

Damma is played on an 8×8 grid of alternating light and dark squares (64 squares total). Play is restricted exclusively to the 32 dark squares.

  • Pieces: Each player begins with exactly 12 pieces placed on the dark squares of the three closest ranks (rows) to them.
  • Setup: Ranks 1–3 for Player 1 (Blue) and Ranks 6–8 for Player 2 (Pink), leaving the center two ranks open.

Ordinary Piece Moves diagonally forward exactly one square into an adjacent dark square. Never moves backward. Jumps over an adjacent opponent piece into an empty diagonal square immediately behind it. Can jump in all 4 diagonal directions.

(Flying King)		 Promoted when reaching the far edge. Moves unlimited diagonal squares (slides) in any direction. Long-Range Jump: Slides from a distance, jumps over a single opponent piece, and can land on any empty dark square beyond it.

4. Mandatory Capture & Multi-Jump Chains [04]

The strategic intensity of Damma resides in its strict capture protocols. This ensures highly interactive board setups and complex sacrifices:

  • Forced Capture: If a player has a valid jump on their turn, they must execute it. Moving another piece is illegal.
  • Multi-Jump Sequences: After completing a diagonal capture, if the active piece has another valid jump from its new landing square, it must perform the next jump in a continuous sequence within the same turn.
  • The Multi-Jump Turn Lock: The turn is locked to the current piece. The player cannot switch to another piece or end the turn until all sequential captures are fully executed.
👑 Rule of the Flying King (Damma) Promotion

When an ordinary piece reaches the opponent's back rank, it is immediately promoted to a Damma. Promotion ends the active turn, even if the square is reached mid-jump sequence. Once promoted, the piece acquires long-range flight capability, letting it slide over multiple empty spaces to target vulnerable opponent lines.

5. Victory Conditions & Technical Architecture [05]

A match concludes immediately when a player is unable to complete a valid move. There are two standard victory pathways:

  • Total Eradication: Capturing all twelve of the opponent's pieces.
  • Positional Lockout: Fully blocking and trapping the opponent's remaining pieces so that they have no legal diagonal moves or jumps remaining.
